# Framework: Content Pillar System
# Scope: Universal framework for organizing content into weighted pillars.
# Usage: Define 3-5 content pillars with weights that sum to 100%.
# This framework explains how pillars work, why weights matter, and how to calibrate.

## 1. What Are Content Pillars?

Content pillars are the 3-5 thematic categories that organize ALL content produced by {COMPANY_NAME}. Every piece of content maps to exactly one pillar. Pillars ensure:

- **Balance**: You don't over-index on one topic and neglect others
- **Strategy**: Content decisions are intentional, not reactive
- **Measurement**: You can track what types of content perform best
- **Consistency**: Your audience knows what to expect from you

## 2. How to Define Pillars

### Step 1: Identify Your Value Propositions

List the top 3-5 things {COMPANY_NAME} offers its audience:
- What products/services do you want to highlight?
- What educational value can you provide?
- What community/cultural value do you bring?
- What unique insights or analysis can you share?

### Step 2: Map to Pillars

Each pillar should represent one value proposition. Recommended structure:

| Pillar | Weight | Content Types | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------------|---------|
| {PILLAR_1} | {WEIGHT_1}% | {CONTENT_TYPES_1} | {PURPOSE_1} |
| {PILLAR_2} | {WEIGHT_2}% | {CONTENT_TYPES_2} | {PURPOSE_2} |
| {PILLAR_3} | {WEIGHT_3}% | {CONTENT_TYPES_3} | {PURPOSE_3} |
| {PILLAR_4} | {WEIGHT_4}% | {CONTENT_TYPES_4} | {PURPOSE_4} |

**Weights must sum to 100%.**

### Step 3: Assign Content Types

Each pillar should have 3-5 specific content types. Examples:
- Product updates, metrics dashboards, launch announcements
- Technical deep-dives, tutorials, how-to guides
- Industry analysis, trend commentary, competitor landscape
- Memes, polls, community milestones, AMAs

## 3. Weight Guidelines

### Starting Weights

If you're unsure, start with this distribution:
- **Primary product/service pillar**: 35-45%
- **Industry/ecosystem intelligence**: 20-30%
- **Education/how-to content**: 20-30%
- **Community/culture**: 5-15%

### Why Weights Matter

- Weights prevent you from becoming a one-note brand (all product shilling or all memes)
- They force you to create diverse content that serves different audience needs
- They give you a benchmark to measure against: "Are we actually producing content at these ratios?"

### Common Mistakes

- **Too much product content** (>50%): Audience tunes out. You become a corporate feed.
- **Too much culture/memes** (>25%): You entertain but don't convert. Brand authority erodes.
- **Equal weights**: Having all pillars at 25% is a sign you haven't prioritized. Some pillars should be heavier.
- **Too many pillars** (>5): Dilutes focus. If you have 6+ themes, consolidate.

If operating multiple accounts (see satellite-account-pattern.md), each account should weight pillars differently:

|---------|------------|------------|------------|------------|
| {MAIN_ACCOUNT} | Standard weights (40/25/25/10) | | | |

Each account's content mix should reflect its persona's emphasis while still touching all pillars.

## 5. Anchor Metrics

Each pillar should have 1-3 anchor metrics that get referenced repeatedly across content:

| Pillar | Anchor Metrics |
|--------|---------------|
| {PILLAR_1} | {METRIC_1}, {METRIC_2} |
| {PILLAR_2} | {METRIC_3}, {METRIC_4} |
| {PILLAR_3} | {METRIC_5}, {METRIC_6} |

Anchor metrics create narrative consistency: "We bridged $X this week" or "Our user base grew to Y."

## 6. Calibration Process

### Weekly Check

Every week, compare actual content output to target weights:
- Count pieces of content per pillar for the past week
- Calculate actual percentages
- If any pillar is off by more than 10% from target, adjust next week's content plan

### Monthly Calibration

Using voice learnings data:
- Which pillar's content gets the most engagement?
- Which pillar's content gets shared most?
- Which pillar drives the most conversions (profile visits, follows, sign-ups)?
- Adjust weights based on performance, following the voice calibration loop triggers

### Quarterly Review

- Are the pillars still relevant? Has the product or market shifted?
- Should any pillar be added, removed, or merged?
- Are the weights still serving the business goals?

## 7. Content Calendar Integration

When planning a weekly content calendar:

1. Calculate how many pieces of content you'll produce this week (e.g., 10 posts)
2. Multiply by each pillar's weight to get target count:
   - {PILLAR_1} at 40%: 4 posts
   - {PILLAR_2} at 25%: 2-3 posts
   - {PILLAR_3} at 25%: 2-3 posts
   - {PILLAR_4} at 10%: 1 post
3. Assign content to specific days and accounts
4. Ensure no single day is dominated by one pillar (variety within each day)
