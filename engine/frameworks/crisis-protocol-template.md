# Framework: Crisis Communication Protocol
# Scope: Universal crisis response template for any company operating social media accounts.
# Usage: Replace {PLACEHOLDER} values with company specifics. Activate this protocol immediately
# when a qualifying crisis event occurs.

## 1. When to Activate

Activate this protocol immediately if ANY of the following occur:

### Universal Triggers (all companies)
- Service disruption affecting users
- Data breach or security incident
- Community backlash, astroturfing accusations, or coordinated attack
- Regulatory action or legal threat
- Competitor attack or disinformation campaign
- Leaked internal documents
- Executive misconduct or PR crisis

### Company-Specific Triggers
Configure these per tenant:
- {COMPANY_TRIGGER_1}: e.g., "Product exploit or vulnerability disclosure"
- {COMPANY_TRIGGER_2}: e.g., "Platform or service deregistration"
- {COMPANY_TRIGGER_3}: e.g., "Partner relationship termination"
- {COMPANY_TRIGGER_N}: Add as many as needed

## 2. Immediate Response (First 30 Minutes)

1. **PAUSE all scheduled content** across ALL accounts immediately (main + all satellites)
2. **Do NOT delete or edit** any prior posts about security, safety, or performance
3. **Do NOT engage** with speculation, FUD, or trolls
4. **Assess scope**: What happened? What is affected? What do we know vs. don't know?
5. **Draft factual acknowledgment** (human-approved ONLY. {REVIEW_AUTHORITY} must approve before posting)

## 3. Response Templates (adapt to situation)

### For technical/product incidents
> We are aware of [specific issue]. [{IMPACT_DESCRIPTION}]. {PRODUCT_NAME} is [paused/operational]. Our team is [specific action being taken]. We will update every [timeframe]. Thread below with details.

### For community/reputational incidents
> We've seen the discussion about [topic]. Here's the context: [factual explanation]. We take this seriously and [specific action]. Happy to answer questions directly.

### For regulatory/legal
> We are aware of [development]. We are reviewing with counsel and will share updates as appropriate. {COMPANY_NAME} remains committed to [compliance/transparency/user protection].

## 4. Per-Account Crisis Behavior

| Account Type | During Crisis |
|-------------|--------------|
| Main account ({MAIN_ACCOUNT}) | ONLY account that posts official updates. Factual, transparent, no spin. |
| Informational satellites | May share ONLY official main account updates via quote-tweet. No independent commentary. |
| Culture/meme satellites | SILENT. No memes, no engagement, no posts. Resume only after all-clear. |

## 5. What NEVER To Do During a Crisis

- Continue posting memes, promotional content, or engagement bait
- Delete or edit prior posts about security, safety, or performance
- Make unverified claims about user safety, fund safety, or data safety
- Promise compensation before assessment is complete
- Go silent for more than 4 hours during an active incident
- Blame users or external parties without verified evidence
- Minimize the situation ("this is nothing," "FUD")
- Let any satellite account freelance. All messaging comes from main account only

## 6. Ongoing Communication

- Update every {UPDATE_INTERVAL} (recommended: every 2-4 hours during active incident)
- Each update should include: what is known, what is being done, what is still unknown, when the next update will come
- If no new information: "No new developments. Our team continues to [action]. Next update at [time]."

## 7. Post-Crisis

### Within 72 Hours
- Publish a post-mortem (factual, technical, transparent)
- Include: timeline of events, root cause, impact, remediation steps, prevention measures
- {REVIEW_AUTHORITY} must approve the post-mortem before publishing

### Resuming Normal Operations
- Update voice learnings file with crisis communication lessons
- Resume satellite account activity gradually:
  1. Informational satellites first
  2. Analysis/education satellites next
  3. Culture/meme satellites last
- Review and update this protocol based on what worked and what did not

### Internal Review
- Conduct internal debrief within 1 week
- Document: what went well, what went poorly, what to change
- Update company-specific triggers if new risk categories were discovered

## 8. Pre-Crisis Preparation

### Standing Resources (prepare before a crisis happens)
- Pre-drafted holding statements for each trigger category
- Designated communication channel for crisis coordination (e.g., dedicated Slack/Discord channel)
- Contact list for {REVIEW_AUTHORITY} and backup decision-makers
- Documented process for pausing all scheduled content across all platforms
- Legal counsel contact information

### Regular Drills
- Quarterly: review and update this protocol
- Annually: run a tabletop crisis simulation with the team
