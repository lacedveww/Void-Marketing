# Framework: Engagement Framework Template
# Scope: Universal structure for reply, DM, and community engagement across platforms.
# Usage: Define response templates (R1-R5) for each engagement scenario.

## 1. Engagement Philosophy

Engagement is not broadcasting. It is participating in conversations. Every reply, DM, or community response should add genuine value. The goal is building relationships, not driving clicks.

## 2. Reply Template System (R1-R5)

Define reply templates by engagement type. Each template is a structure, not a script. It guides tone and content but every reply should be unique.

### R1: Acknowledgment Reply
**When to use:** User shares positive feedback, celebrates a milestone, or tags {COMPANY_NAME}.
**Structure:**
- Acknowledge specifically what they said (not a generic "thanks!")
- Add a relevant detail, insight, or next step
- Keep it under 140 characters for social platforms
**Example pattern:** "Nice. {SPECIFIC_DETAIL_FROM_THEIR_POST}. {RELEVANT_ADDITION}."
**Do NOT:** Use generic phrases like "Great point!", "Love this!", "Thanks for sharing!"

### R2: Informational Reply
**When to use:** User asks a factual question about {COMPANY_NAME} products or the ecosystem.
**Structure:**
- Answer the question directly (first sentence)
- Provide supporting detail or link to documentation
- Include disclaimer if the answer touches on financial topics (per compliance modules)
**Example pattern:** "{DIRECT_ANSWER}. More details: {LINK}. {DISCLAIMER_IF_NEEDED}"

### R3: Engagement Reply
**When to use:** User posts an opinion, analysis, or take relevant to {COMPANY_NAME}'s space.
**Structure:**
- Validate or respectfully challenge their point with a unique perspective
- Add data, a question, or an insight they might not have considered
- Do NOT be promotional. This is a conversation, not a pitch
**Example pattern:** "{ACKNOWLEDGMENT_OF_THEIR_POINT}. {YOUR_UNIQUE_ANGLE_OR_DATA}. {OPTIONAL_QUESTION}"

### R4: Support Reply
**When to use:** User reports an issue, bug, or has a complaint.
**Structure:**
- Acknowledge the issue immediately (do not deflect or minimize)
- Provide a specific next step (DM for details, link to support, escalation path)
- Follow up within the stated timeframe
**Example pattern:** "Thanks for flagging. {ACKNOWLEDGMENT_OF_ISSUE}. DM us / open a ticket at {SUPPORT_LINK} and we'll look into it."

### R5: Deflection Reply
**When to use:** User asks for financial advice, price predictions, or questions that {COMPANY_NAME} cannot answer for compliance reasons.
**Structure:**
- Politely decline to answer the specific question
- Explain why (compliance, not qualified, etc.) without being preachy
- Redirect to appropriate resource or general educational content
**Example pattern:** "Can't give advice on that one. {EDUCATIONAL_REDIRECT}. {DISCLAIMER}"

## 3. DM Engagement Rules

### When to DM
- Only when the user initiates a DM first, OR
- When a public conversation needs to move to private (support issues, account-specific questions)
- NEVER send unsolicited DMs for marketing purposes (violates platform rules)

### DM Templates
- Follow the same R1-R5 framework but with more detail (not character-limited)
- Always include a disclaimer in DMs that involve product or financial topics
- Never request personal information (email, wallet address, etc.) in a DM without explaining why and providing an alternative method

### DM Escalation
- If a DM conversation requires expertise beyond the responder's knowledge, escalate to {ESCALATION_CONTACT}
- Do not guess or provide uncertain answers. It is better to say "Let me check and get back to you"

## 4. Community Platform Engagement (Discord/Telegram)

### Response Priorities
1. Support questions (answer within 1 hour during business hours)
2. Product questions (answer within 4 hours)
3. General discussion (participate naturally, do not dominate)
4. Off-topic chat (light participation, community building)

### Bot vs. Human
- Bot handles: FAQs, automated announcements, moderation
- Human handles: complex questions, emotional situations, complaints, strategic conversations
- When bot confidence is low, defer to human: "Good question. Let me flag this for the team."

## 5. Engagement Metrics to Track

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Reply rate | Are you responding to your community? | >80% of direct questions answered |
| Reply quality score | Are replies substantive? | Track likes/upvotes on replies |
| Average response time | How fast are you? | <4 hours for support, <24 hours for general |
| Conversation depth | Are replies leading to conversations? | >20% of R3 replies get a follow-up |
| Sentiment shift | Are interactions improving perception? | Net positive sentiment trend |

## 6. Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Responding to every single post (looks automated)
- Using the same phrases repeatedly ("Great question!", "Thanks for sharing!")
- Responding to trolls or bad-faith actors (like, mute, or block instead)
- Over-promoting in replies (the reply should serve the conversation, not the brand)
- Responding faster than humanly possible (if automated, add natural delays)
- Ignoring negative feedback (address it directly per R4 template)
