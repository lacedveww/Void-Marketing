# Compliance Module: EU MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)
# Scope: Loads for companies marketing crypto products/services in the EU.
# Usage: Extends `base-rules.md` and `crypto-sec.md`. Adds EU-specific requirements.
# Load when: {COMPANY_MARKETS} includes any EU member state or content is not geo-restricted from EU
# Reference: Regulation (EU) 2023/1114

## 1. Core Marketing Requirements

Under MiCA, all marketing communications for crypto-assets must be:

- **Fair, clear, and not misleading**
- **Identifiable as marketing** (not disguised as editorial or organic content)
- **Consistent with any white paper** published for the crypto-asset
- **Include appropriate risk warnings**

## 2. Required Risk Warnings

All marketing of crypto-assets in the EU must include risk warnings. Recommended text:

> Crypto-assets are highly volatile and may result in the loss of your entire investment. Crypto-assets are not covered by deposit guarantee schemes. Past performance does not guarantee future results.

### Stablecoin-Specific (if applicable)

If marketing stablecoins or asset-referenced tokens:
> This asset-referenced token is not covered by investor compensation or deposit guarantee schemes. The value of this token may fluctuate and holders may lose some or all of their investment.

## 3. Registration Requirement

- Crypto-Asset Service Providers (CASPs) must be registered/authorized under MiCA to market services in the EU
- If {COMPANY_NAME} is not MiCA-registered, marketing to EU residents may be restricted
- Consult legal counsel on whether {COMPANY_NAME}'s services fall under CASP definitions

## 4. Whitepaper Consistency

- If {COMPANY_NAME} has published a crypto-asset white paper under MiCA, all marketing must be consistent with it
- Marketing cannot make claims that contradict or go beyond the white paper
- Marketing must reference the white paper where appropriate

## 5. Reverse Solicitation

- MiCA allows for "reverse solicitation" where a client initiates contact without prior solicitation
- Marketing content (ads, social posts, email campaigns) is NOT reverse solicitation
- Do not rely on reverse solicitation exemptions for active marketing campaigns

## 6. Influencer Marketing

- MiCA's marketing rules apply to influencer content as well
- Influencers promoting crypto-assets on behalf of {COMPANY_NAME} must comply with the same disclosure and risk warning requirements
- {COMPANY_NAME} is responsible for ensuring influencer content meets MiCA standards

## 7. Geographic Considerations

- MiCA applies across all 27 EU member states
- Transition periods may apply: some provisions effective from June 2023, full application from December 2024
- Individual member states may have additional requirements beyond MiCA
- When in doubt, default to the most restrictive interpretation

## 8. Enforcement

National Competent Authorities (NCAs) in each EU member state have the power to:
- Issue warnings and sanctions
- Suspend marketing campaigns
- Impose fines (proportionate to the severity)
- Require corrective statements

For any content targeting EU residents:
