# Compliance Module: UK FCA (Financial Conduct Authority)
# Scope: Loads for companies marketing crypto products/services that may reach UK residents.
# Usage: Extends `base-rules.md` and `crypto-sec.md`. Adds UK-specific requirements.
# Load when: {COMPANY_MARKETS} includes "UK" or content is not geo-restricted from UK
# Reference: FCA PS22/10, effective October 2023

## 1. Core Requirement

ALL crypto promotions that could reach UK residents must include prominent risk warnings.

**Required text (mandatory, must be included as-is or substantially equivalent):**

> Don't invest unless you're prepared to lose all the money you invest.

This is non-negotiable. No exemptions for social media. No exemptions for short-form content.

## 2. Additional FCA Requirements

### 2.1 Risk Warnings Must Be Prominent

- Risk warning must be as prominent as the promotional content itself
- Cannot be buried in fine print, footnotes, or behind a "read more" link
- On social media: risk warning should be in the main post body, not a reply or thread continuation
- For images/video: risk warning must be visible for sufficient duration

### 2.2 Fair, Clear, and Not Misleading

All crypto promotions must be:
- **Fair**: Balanced presentation of benefits and risks
- **Clear**: Plain language, no jargon that obscures risk
- **Not misleading**: No omission of material information

### 2.3 Cooling-Off Period References

- FCA requires that consumers be given a "cooling-off" period for certain crypto purchases
- Marketing must not create artificial urgency that undermines cooling-off rights
- Do not use: "limited time," "act now," "don't miss out," "only X spots left"

### 2.4 Incentives to Invest

- FCA restricts "refer a friend" bonuses and similar incentives for crypto
- Referral programs that offer crypto rewards for bringing new users may violate FCA rules
- Consult legal counsel before launching any referral or incentive program targeting UK users

### 2.5 Approved Persons

- Only FCA-authorized or registered firms may communicate crypto promotions in the UK
- If {COMPANY_NAME} is not FCA-registered, consult legal counsel on whether an exemption applies
- The s21 exemption (communication by authorized persons) may apply if working with an FCA-authorized firm

## 3. Social Media Specifics

### X/Twitter Posts Targeting UK
- Include risk warning IN the tweet body (not just in bio or pinned tweet)
- For threads: risk warning in the first tweet of the thread
- Short-form version (if character-limited): "Capital at risk. Don't invest unless prepared to lose all."

### Blog Posts / Long-Form
- Full FCA risk warning prominently displayed near the top of the article
- Risk warning repeated adjacent to any call-to-action

### Video / Audio
- Spoken risk warning at the start and/or end
- Written risk warning in video description / show notes

## 4. Enforcement

FCA has the power to:
- Issue cease-and-desist orders for non-compliant promotions
- Fine companies up to unlimited amounts for breaches
- Refer cases for criminal prosecution in serious cases
- Order takedown of social media content

## 5. Implementation Checklist

For any content that could reach UK residents:
- [ ] Contains the mandatory FCA risk warning text?
- [ ] Risk warning is as prominent as the promotional content?
- [ ] No artificial urgency language?
- [ ] No incentives to invest (refer-a-friend bonuses, etc.) without legal review?
- [ ] Fair, clear, and not misleading?
- [ ] If {COMPANY_NAME} is not FCA-registered, has an exemption been confirmed?
