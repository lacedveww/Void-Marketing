# LinkedIn Format Decision: Posts vs. Articles

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Decision:** Use regular LinkedIn posts (under 3,000 characters), NOT long-form articles.

## Research Summary

### The Data Against Articles

- LinkedIn articles have the **second-lowest organic reach** of all content formats on the platform, only beating external link posts.
- Regular feed posts generate **2-4x higher engagement rates** than articles (source: ContentIn 2026 benchmarks, SocialInsider 2025 benchmarks).
- LinkedIn's algorithm in 2025-2026 prioritizes native feed content (carousels, multi-image, text posts) over articles, which live on a separate publishing layer.
- Articles do not appear in the main feed by default; they require a click-through, adding friction that kills reach.
- Organic reach for company pages dropped 60-66% from 2024 to 2026. Articles were hit hardest since they already had lower distribution.

### Optimal Post Length

- **Sweet spot: 1,300-1,900 characters** for maximum engagement (ConnectSafely 2026, AutoPosting 2025, Digital Blacksmiths 2025).
- Posts up to 3,000 characters still perform well for technical/thought-leadership content that needs depth.
- The first 210-235 characters (above the "See More" fold) are critical for hook quality.
- Posts that keep readers engaged (dwell time) get algorithmic boosts, so dense, valuable content outperforms padded content.

### What Works for B2B Technical Content

- Quality over quantity: content with concrete data points, real examples, and actionable insights generates **3.7x more reach** than generalized content (Brixon Group 2026).
- LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes vague, overly commercial content and rewards usefulness, originality, and relevance.
- Comments are **15x more valuable than likes** for algorithmic distribution.
- Personal profiles drive **561% more reach** than company pages for the same content.

### Why Articles Still Have Some Advocates

- Articles have longer shelf life (searchable, indexed by Google).
- They generate deeper, more thoughtful comments from senior professionals.
- They serve as reference material for existing followers.

However, for VoidAI's goals (building awareness, driving bridge/SN106 adoption, establishing thought leadership with new audiences), reach and engagement volume matter more than archival permanence. The content can always be repurposed as blog posts on docs.voidai.com for SEO/permanence.

## Recommendation

**Trim all 6 LinkedIn content items to under 3,000 characters as regular posts.**

Target range: 1,800-2,800 characters to allow for technical depth while staying within the post format. VoidAI's content is technical/institutional, so slightly longer posts (toward the upper end) are appropriate for the audience.

Preserve:
- Opening hooks (first 210 chars must grab attention)
- Key data points and metrics
- Compliance disclaimers (required, non-negotiable)
- CTAs and links

Cut:
- Excessive section headers and horizontal rules (article formatting)
- Redundant explanations
- "What We Did Not Build" style tangents
- Repeated metrics across posts
