**Bittensor's First Halving: What It Means for the Network**

On December 15, 2025, Bittensor completed its first halving. Daily emissions dropped from 7,200 TAO to 3,600 TAO. Annual inflation fell from ~26% to 13%.

Three months later, the data tells a clear story.

**The Mechanics**

Unlike Bitcoin's time-based halvings, Bittensor's is supply-triggered. It activated when circulating supply crossed 10.5M TAO (half of 21M max). Next halving: estimated ~2029. Faster adoption accelerates the supply curve. Slower growth delays it.

**Network State Post-Halving**

The network absorbed the reduction without structural disruption:
- TAO holding a top-40 CMC rank with a multi-billion dollar market cap (check current price on CoinGecko)
- Majority of TAO staked. Holders locking supply, not selling
- 128+ active subnets (at capacity, with competitive replacement)
- TAO ranks #2 in AI coin mentions as of Q3 2025 (LunarCrush)
- Grayscale filed for a Bittensor Trust in December 2025

Pre-halving Q2 2025 growth: +50% subnets, +16% miners, +28% wallets, +21.5% staked TAO. The halving did not reverse these trends.

**Impact on Subnet Economics**

3,600 TAO/day across 128+ subnets. Each subnet's share depends on TAO staked to it under Dynamic TAO (dTAO). Subnets that fail to attract stake receive progressively smaller emissions.

Low-performing subnets can be deregistered after a 4-month immunity period. With emissions halved, the cost of underperformance doubled. The message for subnet operators: demonstrate measurable output or lose your position.

**What This Means**

For holders: reduced new supply and a high staking participation rate signal long-term network commitment. Staking outcomes are variable, not guaranteed.

For builders: institutional attention (Grayscale), reduced inflation, and 128+ active subnets create a more stable foundation. The question shifts from "will this network survive" to "which subnets will sustain real usage."

For miners/validators: competition intensifies. Smaller pool means performance scoring has larger economic consequences.
