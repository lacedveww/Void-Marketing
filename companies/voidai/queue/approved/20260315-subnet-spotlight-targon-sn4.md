## Thread Parts

### Part 1
Subnet Spotlight: Targon (SN4)

Deterministic verification for LLM inference on Bittensor. One of the oldest subnets, consistently in the top 10 by mindshare.

### Part 2
The core problem: how do you verify that a miner actually ran the model you asked for?

Most inference subnets use statistical sampling. Targon uses deterministic verification. Same input, same seed, same output. If a miner's response doesn't match, they're caught.

### Part 3
Why this matters: without reliable verification, miners can serve cheaper, lower-quality models and pocket the difference.

Targon's approach creates a provable inference layer. Validators can confirm, with certainty, that the model ran correctly.

### Part 4
SN4 has been running since Bittensor's early subnet era. That longevity means battle-tested validation logic and a mature miner network.

Newer inference subnets often reference Targon's verification patterns as a benchmark.

### Part 5
If you're evaluating Bittensor's inference stack, SN4 is where deterministic verification lives. The subnet that other subnets measure themselves against.
